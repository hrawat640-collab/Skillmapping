How to use SkillMapper

Turn a plain English description of what you need into the exact job title, salary range, and candidate profile — in seconds.

1

Three ways to find any role

SkillMapper has 188 roles across 18 departments. Pick the entry point that matches what you know:

Add skills — you know the skills needed but not the exact title. Type skills one by one and see which roles match.

Browse by title — you know the job title. Search or scroll the full role list, select one or more, and instantly see skills + salary.

Describe what you need — you know the problem but not the role. Type in plain English and SkillMapper identifies the right title.
2

Read the result cards

Each card shows the required skills (non-negotiable), nice-to-have skills, salary by level in INR and USD, and an amber hiring tip with context specific to the role.

Click Junior / Mid / Senior / Lead on the salary selector to see the experience range and pay for each level. Leave the level empty in your JD if you're open across levels.

💰 Salary data: Glassdoor India, AmbitionBox, Naukri — Q4 2025 benchmarks. INR = CTC (base + PF + variable). Verify at ambitionbox.com before making an offer.
3

Read what SkillMapper tells you

Every result card shows you four things: what the role actually does (plain English description), the skills required at that level, what market salary to expect by Junior / Mid / Senior / Lead, and a smart hiring tip specific to that role — when to hire it, what to watch for, common mistakes.

The amber tip box on each card is the most underused part of SkillMapper. It answers the questions no JD tells you — like "don't hire a Data Architect at 3 years experience" or "for SAP, always specify the module or you'll get irrelevant applications."
4

Source the candidate in TalentXRay

Once you have the right role, click "Search in TalentXRay". It opens pre-filled with the job title and top skills — ready to find real candidate profiles on LinkedIn, GitHub, Stack Overflow, or Behance via Google X-Ray search.

No LinkedIn Recruiter licence needed. The full workflow takes under 2 minutes from role identification to Google search results.

Pro tips
🎯

Be specific about the problem

Don't say "marketing person" — say "grow our user base organically through content and SEO". The more specific the description, the better the match.

🔢

Match % is relative to your input

60% with 2 skills entered is a strong signal. 20% with 8 skills entered is weak. More skills entered = stricter scoring = more meaningful percentages.

⚠️

Read the amber tip on every card

These tips are written from real JD patterns. They tell you when a role is right for your stage, what to add to your search, and when founders typically over- or under-hire.

💼

Check alternative titles

The "Also called" section shows all the different names for the same role. Use these in TalentXRay to cast a wider net — candidates use different titles for the same job.

💰

Salaries vary by city

Bangalore, Mumbai, and Delhi pay 20–40% above the ranges shown. Hyderabad and Pune are roughly at the mid-point. Remote roles follow Bangalore rates in most cases.

📊

Use the dept filter for broad searches

If you described something like "data person" and got 6 matches, use the Data department filter to see only data roles, then compare them side by side.

About the data

Salary data — how we built it and what it means. The ranges you see are market estimates built from publicly available sources: Glassdoor India, AmbitionBox, Naukri, and iimjobs for India ranges; Levels.fyi, Glassdoor US/UK, LinkedIn Salary, and Blind for global ranges. We did not verify these against any proprietary database or conduct a formal compensation survey. They represent patterns we observed across public data up to early 2026. Actual compensation varies significantly by company size, city, funding stage, and candidate profile — treat these as a starting point, not a source of truth.

We are crowdsourcing better data. When you click Mid, Senior, or Lead salary levels, we invite you to share your own CTC anonymously. Every submission helps us move from estimates to real percentile data. Once we have enough submissions per role, we will show 25th and 95th percentile ranges sourced directly from professionals like you. Your data is stored anonymously — no name, no email, just role, experience band, CTC, currency, and country.

Cross-reference before acting. We recommend verifying with AmbitionBox, Glassdoor, or Levels.fyi before making or evaluating an offer. INR figures are CTC (base + PF + variable). USD figures are total compensation at growth-stage companies.

Role database covers 200+ roles across Engineering, Data, Design, Product, Marketing, Sales, HR, Finance, Operations, and Legal. Role definitions, required skills, and experience bands are derived from patterns across thousands of real JDs and public salary data up to Q1 2026.

Skills database contains 1,400+ skills across 74 categories — covering tech, non-tech, industry-specific, and emerging skills. Last updated: Q1 2026.

Privacy & Data Policy

Plain English. No legal jargon. Here is exactly what we collect, why, and what you can do about it.

📋
Last updated: April 2026
This policy applies to users in India, the United States, the United Kingdom, Ireland, Poland, and all other countries. Where laws differ, we follow the stricter standard.
Who we are
SkillMapper is a free tool built and operated by Himani Rawat under the brand Talents Radar, based in Pune, India. You can reach us at contactus@talentsradar.com for any privacy-related request.

We are not a company registered under GDPR as a formal data controller — we are an independent tool operator. However, we voluntarily follow GDPR principles because our users include people from the UK, EU, and US, and we believe it is the right thing to do.
What we collect and why
✓ Email address
Why: To give you access to the tool and send verification codes. We do not sell your email or share it with third parties. If you opted in, we may send occasional product updates — you can unsubscribe any time.
✓ Name and profession type
Why: To personalise your experience (e.g. showing "Find professionals" vs "Search candidates" based on whether you are a fresher or HR). We do not use this for advertising.
✓ Salary data (if you choose to share)
Why: To improve salary benchmarks for everyone. This is entirely voluntary. We store it without your name attached — only role, experience band, CTC, currency, and country. You cannot be identified from this data.
✓ CV evaluation results (anonymised)
Why: To understand which roles people evaluate most and improve our skill database. We store only the match score and role title — never your CV text, never your name linked to results.
✗ What we never collect
We never collect payment information, government IDs, passport numbers, health data, or any sensitive personal data. We never run advertising on this tool. We never sell any data to anyone.
Your CV — what actually happens to it
When you upload or paste your CV, the text is processed entirely within your browser. Your CV content never leaves your device and is never sent to our servers or any third party.

The only thing we log is: which role you evaluated against, your match score (e.g. 72%), how many skills were found, and how many were missing. This is completely anonymous — there is no way to trace it back to you.

We occasionally extract skill names that appear in CVs but are not yet in our database — for example a new tool or framework — so we can add them. We never read your personal details (name, address, employer, salary in your CV).
Cookies and local storage
We do not use tracking cookies. We do not use advertising cookies.

We use local storage (stored on your device, not sent anywhere) to remember that you are logged in so you do not have to verify your email every time. This includes your name and email address, stored locally on your browser. You can clear this at any time by signing out or clearing your browser data.

We use Google Analytics to count page visits and understand which features are used most. Google Analytics collects anonymised usage data (pages visited, time on page, country). It does not identify you personally. You can opt out using the Google Analytics Opt-out browser add-on.
Where your data is stored
Our database runs on Supabase, hosted on AWS infrastructure in Singapore. Supabase is SOC 2 Type II certified and follows industry-standard security practices. You can read Supabase's own data processing agreement at supabase.com/privacy.

For users in the UK and EU: we acknowledge that this means your data is stored outside the UK/EU (in Singapore). We take steps to ensure adequate protection consistent with UK GDPR and EU GDPR principles, including using providers with appropriate certifications.
