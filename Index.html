How to use SkillMapper

Turn a plain English description of what you need into the exact job title, salary range, and candidate profile — in seconds.

1

Describe what you need in plain English

Use the "Describe what you need" tab and type naturally — exactly how you'd say it to a friend. Don't try to use HR jargon or technical terms.

✅ Works great: "I need someone to manage our Salesforce and automate our sales workflows"
✅ Works great: "we want to grow our product on social media and run paid ads"
✅ Works great: "need a backend developer to build APIs for our app"
❌ Too vague: "tech person" · "marketing" · "developer"
2

Or add skills directly if you know them

Switch to the "Add skills directly" tab to type specific skills one by one. Press Enter after each skill. The typeahead shows suggestions as you type — click to select.

Start broad — add 2–3 skills and see what roles appear, then add more to refine. You don't need to know every skill for the role.

Try typing: Python → Enter → Spark → Enter → Airflow → Enter
Result: Data Engineer at 60%+ match
3

Read the match cards carefully

Each result shows you the % skill match, experience levels with year ranges, salary bands in both INR and USD, and alternative titles people use for the same role.

The amber tip on each card gives you context — when to hire this role, what to add to narrow the search, and common mistakes founders make with this hire.

💰 Salary note: INR figures = CTC (base + PF + bonus). USD figures = total comp for US/UK/Canada. Data updated Q4 2025 from Naukri, LinkedIn, AmbitionBox, and levels.fyi. Verify current ranges at ambitionbox.com (India) or levels.fyi (global).
4

Filter by department

When multiple roles match, use the department filter chips that appear above results. Click Engineering, Marketing, Data, or any other department to narrow the view.

If you see 8+ matches, you've described something too broad — try being more specific in your description or add more skills.

5

Go find them in TalentXRay

Once you've identified the right role, click "Search in TalentXRay" on the result card. TalentXRay opens pre-filled with the job title, alternative names, and matched skills already in the search fields — so you can start sourcing immediately.

The complete workflow: describe need in SkillMapper → identify role → click Search in TalentXRay → Google X-Ray finds real profiles → Chrome extension gets contact details.

Pro tips
🎯

Be specific about the problem

Don't say "marketing person" — say "grow our user base organically through content and SEO". The more specific the description, the better the match.

🔢

Match % is relative to your input

60% with 2 skills entered is a strong signal. 20% with 8 skills entered is weak. More skills entered = stricter scoring = more meaningful percentages.

⚠️

Read the amber tip on every card

These tips are written from real JD patterns. They tell you when a role is right for your stage, what to add to your search, and when founders typically over- or under-hire.

💼

Check alternative titles

The "Also called" section shows all the different names for the same role. Use these in TalentXRay to cast a wider net — candidates use different titles for the same job.

💰

Salaries vary by city

Bangalore, Mumbai, and Delhi pay 20–40% above the ranges shown. Hyderabad and Pune are roughly at the mid-point. Remote roles follow Bangalore rates in most cases.

📊

Use the dept filter for broad searches

If you described something like "data person" and got 6 matches, use the Data department filter to see only data roles, then compare them side by side.

About the data

Salary data is sourced from Naukri, LinkedIn, AmbitionBox, Glassdoor India, iimjobs (India) and levels.fyi, Glassdoor US, Glassdoor UK, and Blind (global). Figures represent Q4 2025 market benchmarks. INR = CTC (Cost to Company, includes base + PF + variable). USD = total compensation at growth-stage companies.

Role database covers 48 roles across Engineering, Data, Design, Product, Marketing, Sales, HR, Finance, Operations, and Salesforce/CRM. Role definitions, required skills, and experience bands are derived from patterns across thousands of real JDs analysed up to Q4 2025.

Skills database contains 1,403 skills across 74 categories — covering tech, non-tech, industry-specific, and emerging skills. Last updated: Q4 2025.
